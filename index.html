Coach Chat uses Mistral, an AI model, to give you open-ended coaching answers and adapt your plan in real time. Before you turn it on, here's what to know.

What gets shared: Your training plan, recent activities, and check-in data go to Mistral when you send a message. Your name doesn't.

Where it goes: Mistral is hosted in the EU. Adapt sends your data through its own server — no one else sees it.

What it costs you: Nothing. Adapt covers the API cost during the prototype (capped at 50 messages/day per user).

What it can do: Move sessions, shorten days, flag injuries, scale volume — and explain why. It cannot diagnose injuries or give medical/nutrition advice.

Troubleshoot ▾
If sync isn't working, run Diagnose. The most common fix is to Re-authorize and tick the "View data about your private activities" checkbox on the Strava consent screen.
📋 First-time setup — show me how (3 minutes)
You'll need a free Strava account. The "API app" you're about to create is a one-time thing that lets Adapt read your training history.
1
Open strava.com/settings/api in a new tab and sign in.
2
If it's your first time, you'll need to create an API app. Fill in any name (e.g. "Adapt"), any website (your Strava profile is fine), and for Authorization Callback Domain enter exactly:
adapt-prototype-aiu3.vercel.app
That last one is critical — getting it wrong is the most common reason setup fails.
3
Strava shows you a Client ID (a number) and Client Secret (a long string — you may need to tap "Show" to reveal it). Copy both into the fields below.
4
Tap Authorize via Strava below. Strava will open a consent screen.
5
CRITICAL: on the consent screen, scroll through the permissions list and tick the box for "View data about your private activities and segment efforts." If you skip this, sync silently fails — Strava will return only public data, which most users don't have. Then tap the orange Authorize button.
6
Strava redirects you back to Adapt and starts pulling your activity history. First sync takes ~10-30 seconds depending on how much you've logged.
Stuck? Tap the "Diagnose" button (visible after you connect) — it'll tell you whether your token has the right scope, or open strava.com/settings/apps and revoke "Adapt", then retry from step 4.
New here? Tap 📋 First-time setup above for a 3-minute walkthrough.

Help & FAQ

How do I change my plan?
Tap any session on Today or Plan to view details. From there, tap Edit. Or talk to the chat: "move Saturday to Sunday."
What does my readiness score mean?
0-100 scale. 85+ is on track. 65-84 means small things to tighten up. Below 65 means real risks. Tap the pill anywhere to see the full breakdown.
What if I'm injured?
Say "my [area] hurts" in chat, or log it at morning check-in. Engine auto-adjusts. See a doctor or PT for actual medical advice — the app flags, it doesn't diagnose.
Can't run today — what do I do?
Tap "Adjust today" on the workout card. Deselect the disciplines you can't do. Workout adapts automatically.
My watch data isn't showing up
This prototype doesn't actually connect to watches yet — data is simulated. In production, you'd link Apple Health or Garmin Connect in Profile → Data sources.
How do notifications work?
When your logged workout data suggests an adjustment, a notification appears on the bell. Tap Accept to apply, Reject to dismiss.
What's Quick Actions vs Coach Chat?
Quick Actions is free and handles plan changes + common training questions with scripted answers. Coach Chat uses Claude AI for open-ended coaching conversations — requires your own Anthropic API key.
I want to start over
Profile tab → "Start over with a new plan." Your current plan resets, you go through onboarding again.

    About Coach Chat

    Open-ended AI coaching that can read and reshape your plan.

    What it does. Ask the coach anything about your training — why a session is structured the way it is, how to adapt when life interferes, what to expect on race day. It can also execute changes: move a session, shorten a day, flag an injury, scale a build week.

    What it sees. Each time you send a message, Adapt sends your current plan, recent Strava activities, and check-in data to Mistral (the AI). Your name and login don't go.

    What it doesn't do. No medical diagnosis, no nutrition prescriptions, no gear advice. It will redirect those.

    Daily cap. 50 messages per day per device, to keep API costs sane during the prototype.

    Reset. You can clear the conversation and turn off Coach Chat anytime in your Account screen.

    Privacy: Adapt forwards your messages and plan context to Mistral's API. Mistral states it doesn't train on API traffic. Conversation history is stored only in your browser.
